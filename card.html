African Elephant

Mother elephant with her calf

At birth, baby elephant calves weigh 250 pounds and are three feet tall. They can't see very well at first, but can recognize their mothers using touch, scent, and sound.

  • Genus: Loxodonta
  • Species: africana
  • Size: 6-13 ft at the shoulder
  • Toenails: Forefeet (4), hindfeet (3)

African elephants inhabit the sub-Saharan regions of Africa. Savanna Elephants and Forest Elephants are the two subspecies of African elephants. Their trunks have two nostrils, while Asian elephants have only one. African Elephants have consistant coloration and no humps on their heads while Asian elephants are spotted and have humps on their heads.
